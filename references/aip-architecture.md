# AIP Architecture

## Purpose

This is a concise, platform-safe adaptation of the strategic method in `wechat-aip-architect`. It preserves the operating model rather than fixed platform figures, historic availability, or tool-specific layouts.

The order is **positioning → account setup → benchmark → topic → writing → format**. A later step cannot repair an undefined reader, empty proof, or missing human decision.

## Five permanent bottom lines

1. Platform rules, creator thresholds, commercial permissions, and API access are time-sensitive; verify a current primary source before relying on them.
2. Never promise income, readership, follower growth, recommendation, conversion, or eligibility.
3. Benchmark learning is not copying: do not reuse expressive language, visual work, screenshots, proprietary material, results, or implied endorsement.
4. Before advertising, affiliate commerce, paid products, courses, services, or lead collection, check qualifications, truthfulness, industry restrictions, and current platform rules.
5. Keep credentials out of content artifacts, screenshots, source repositories, and generated HTML. Use the user's approved secret-management method.

## Phase 1 — Positioning: person, offer, scene

| Dimension | Decision |
| --- | --- |
| Person | What lived experience, work context, skill, learning journey, and point of view make the account credible? |
| Offer | What useful knowledge, service, product hypothesis, or decision help can be truthfully supplied? |
| Scene | Where does the reader receive value and, if appropriate, where may compliant conversion happen? |

Write a one-sentence account promise: **who it serves, the recurring problem, and the useful outcome**. Start narrow enough that a reader can recognise it. Trust and a recognisable human point of view precede monetization; this is a strategy principle, not a platform-algorithm claim.

## Phase 2 — Account setup

Make visible signals match the promise:

- A clear, accountable name that communicates topic and creator identity where appropriate.
- A bio that leads with the reader's benefit.
- An avatar and visual identity with a clear rights decision.
- A topic boundary: what the account covers and deliberately does not cover.
- An honest content and conversion path.

Keep a monetization hypothesis separate from a promise. Support, qualified affiliate commerce, advertising, paid content, owned products, and services are possible paths, but each requires current eligibility, suitability, and evidence checks before activation.

## Phase 3 — Benchmark

Use three levels:

1. **Account:** positioning, voice, content rhythm, and trust/product path.
2. **Article:** reader problem, headline promise, structure, evidence, and conclusion.
3. **Element:** opening, emotional movement, insight shift, interaction, visual treatment, and next action.

For a high-value sample, review headline pull, opening pull, flow, usable value, and honest action. Then look for emotional movement, new insight, and action trigger. Identify the mismatch or weakness before adapting anything.

## Phase 4 — Topic

Topic candidates may come from the creator's real work and learning, consented reader questions, carefully documented benchmark patterns, and current primary sources. Each candidate needs a reader question, account-specific angle, useful outcome, proof available, proof missing, and originality decision.

## Phase 5 — Writing and original transformation

1. Open with verified outcome, verified value, reader pain, or honest curiosity.
2. Deliver a method, decision, or limitation a reader can actually use.
3. Humanize with a real story, failure, judgment, or learning moment that clarifies the topic.
4. Close with a truthful interaction, summary, or clearly scoped resource.

If a benchmark inspired structure, transform it through **audience, context, evidence, and voice**. Add original tests, observed limits, or account-specific judgment. Without those, keep it as research.

For AI-assisted editing, create an AIP voice profile from the creator's own approved writing. Comparative review may analyse structural differences with a benchmark; it may not reuse expressive language or proprietary content.

## Phase 6 — Format

Use exactly one final HTML lane. Formatting improves readability and visual identity; it must not fabricate proof, change locked claims, or silently publish.
